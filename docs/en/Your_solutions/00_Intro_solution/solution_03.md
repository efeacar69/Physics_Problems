## 3. Proportionality (Universal Law of Gravitation)

### Problem Statement
How does the gravitational force $F$ change if the distance $r$ is *doubled* and both masses $m_1, m_2$ are *halved*?


### Analytical Derivation
We start with Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

*Let the new parameters be:*
•⁠  ⁠New masses: $m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$
•⁠  ⁠New distance: $r' = 2r$

*Substituting these into the formula:*
$$F' = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

*Simplifying the expression:*
1.⁠ ⁠The numerator scale factor is $\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$
2.⁠ ⁠The denominator scale factor is $2^2 = 4$

Therefore:
$$F' = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2} = \frac{1/4}{4} \cdot \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$
$$F' = \frac{1}{16} F$$


### Conclusion
The gravitational force *decreases by a factor of 16*.
