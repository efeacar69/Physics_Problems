## 5. Trigonometry (Vector Components)

### Problem Statement
A vector $\vec{A}$ has a magnitude of 15 and makes an angle of $\theta = 60^\circ$ with the horizontal axis. Calculate its horizontal ($A_x$) and vertical ($A_y$) components.


### Mathematical Derivation
We use the trigonometric definitions for a right-angled triangle where the vector is the hypotenuse.

*1. Horizontal Component ($A_x$):*
Using the cosine function:
$$A_x = A \cos(\theta)$$
$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$
Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5$$

*2. Vertical Component ($A_y$):*
Using the sine function:
$$A_y = A \sin(\theta)$$
$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$
Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:
$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx 12.99$$


### Final Result
The components of the vector are:
*$A_x = 7.5, \quad A_y \approx 12.99$*
