## 7. Logic & Series (The Fly Problem)

### Problem Analysis
This problem can be viewed as an infinite geometric series; however, the most efficient method is to analyze the *total time* of the system.


### Calculation

*1. Time until the bicycle reaches the wall:*
The bicycle travels at a constant speed ($v = 1 \text{ m/s}$) over a distance of $10 \text{ meters}$.
$$t = \frac{d}{v} = \frac{10 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ m/s}} = 10 \text{ seconds}$$

*2. Total distance traveled by the fly:*
The fly travels at a constant speed ($v_{fly} = 2 \text{ m/s}$) for the entire duration of the bicycle's trip. It does not matter how many times it reverses direction.
$$D = v_{fly} \times t$$
$$D = 2 \text{ m/s} \times 10 \text{ s} = 20 \text{ meters}$$


### Conclusion
The total distance the fly travels before the impact is *20 meters*. This demonstrates that while the path is complex, the physical duration provides a straightforward solution.
