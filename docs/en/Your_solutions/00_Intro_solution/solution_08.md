## 8. Definite Integrals (Area Under Curve)

### Problem Statement
Calculate the area under the curve of $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x = 0$ to $x = \pi$.


### Integration Process
The area $A$ is given by the definite integral:
$$A = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx$$

*Step 1: Find the antiderivative*
The antiderivative of $\sin(x)$ is $-\cos(x)$.

*Step 2: Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus*
$$A = [-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}$$
$$A = (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$$

*Step 3: Evaluate the values*
Since $\cos(\pi) = -1$ and $\cos(0) = 1$:
$$A = (-(-1)) - (-1) = 1 + 1 = 2$$


### Final Result
The total area under the curve is exactly *2*.
