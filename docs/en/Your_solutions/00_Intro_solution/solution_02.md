## 2. Systems of Equations

### Problem Statement
Find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following system of equations:
1.⁠ ⁠$2x + 3y = 12$
2.⁠ ⁠$x - y = 1$


### Method: Substitution
I have chosen the *Substitution Method* due to the simplicity of the second equation.

*Step 1: Isolate $x$ from Equation (2)*
By rearranging the second equation, we get:
$$x = y + 1$$

*Step 2: Substitute into Equation (1)*
Now, we substitute this expression for $x$ into the first equation:
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

*Step 3: Solve for $y$*
Expanding the terms and simplifying:
$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
$$5y = 10 \implies y = 2$$

*Step 4: Find the value of $x$*
Substitute $y = 2$ back into our isolated expression:
$$x = 2 + 1 = 3$$


### Verification
To ensure the result is correct:
$2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$ (Matches Equation 1)

*Final Answer:*
The solution set is *$x = 3, \quad y = 2$*.
